03 Project

Impedance Matching

RF EngineeringImpedance MatchingS-ParametersSmith Chart
Problem
High reflected power was limiting plasma performance, and the relationship between stub tuning and impedance transformation was not well understood.
Approach
Self-directed study of matching networks, S-parameters, and Smith chart methods. Modeled 3- and 4-stub tuner behavior to develop a systematic tuning methodology.
Outcome
Reduced reflected power from 30% to <1% at 6kW. Methodology applied at pilot scale with the same result; informed procurement of automatic tuners.

The problem with reflected power

In microwave plasma systems, impedance mismatch between the generator and the load causes a portion of the input power to reflect back toward the source rather than coupling into the plasma. At bench scale, this was limiting achievable power and causing process instability.

Learning the theory

I conducted self-directed research into RF and microwave engineering fundamentals to understand what was physically happening:

  • Matching networks and how they transform load impedance
  • S-parameters and the reflection coefficient (S11)
  • The Smith chart as a graphical tool for visualizing impedance transformation
  • The physics of 3-stub tuners and how stub position rotates load impedance on the Smith chart

I used a 3-stub tuner model to understand the behavior of a 4-stub configuration used in our system.

Results

Reflected power reduced from ~30% to <1% at 6kW bench scale.

The same methodology was applied to the 915MHz pilot reactor with consistent results. These findings also directly informed the decision to invest in automatic tuners — and gave me the foundation to operate and troubleshoot them when sensors failed or needed recalibration.
