01 Project

Microwave Plasma Experiments

Microwave Plasma Experimental Design Scale-up RF Systems
Problem
Evaluate performance, stability, and product quality of bench-scale microwave plasma for dry reforming and pyrolysis chemistries.
Approach
Owned and operated the 2.45GHz test skid end-to-end. Designed experiments to measure conversion, efficiency, power absorption, arcing, and product morphology.
Outcome
Scaled bench reactor from 1kW to 6kW. Impedance matching insights applied at pilot scale enabled stable 100kW operation.

What I measured

Experiments evaluated three categories of performance:

  • Performance — conversion efficiency and power absorption
  • Stability — arcing behavior, reflection coefficient, time-to-steady-state (thermal and compositional)
  • Product quality — morphology, particle size, crystallinity, and impurities

Key findings

Notable experiments on reflected power and arcing revealed that impedance mismatch was causing instability at higher power levels. These findings drove targeted reactor modifications — changes to geometry and tuner configuration — that significantly improved power transfer.

Results & scale-up impact

The bench program successfully scaled from 1kW to 6kW for both dry reforming and pyrolysis chemistries. The impedance matching methodology developed at bench scale was directly applied to the 915MHz pilot reactor, enabling stable operation at full 100kW — a 100× increase in power.
