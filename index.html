Work

Selected projects & case studies.

01 Project

Microwave Plasma Experiments

Ran bench-scale microwave plasma experiments for dry reforming and pyrolysis to evaluate performance, stability, and product quality. Owned and operated the 2.45GHz test skid end-to-end, with key findings on reflected power and arcing driving reactor modifications. Scaled from 1kW to 6kW; impedance matching insights carried to the 915MHz pilot, enabling stable operation at 100kW.

Microwave Plasma Experimental Design Scale-up RF Systems
02 Project

Plasma Reactor SCADA

Maintained and developed SCADA systems for bench and pilot-scale plasma reactors. Integrated new sensors, redesigned the front panel for operator clarity, and built a safety interlock system with alarm and automatic fault-shutdown logic. Improved observability reduced monitoring fatigue and enabled autonomous operation.

LabVIEW cRIO Instrumentation Safety Systems
03 Project

Impedance Matching

Investigated RF impedance matching to maximize power transfer into plasma reactors. Studied matching networks, S-parameters, and Smith chart methods; modeled 3- and 4-stub tuner behavior. Reduced reflected power from 30% to <1% at 6kW bench scale — methodology applied at pilot scale with the same result, and informed procurement of automatic tuners.

RF Engineering Impedance Matching S-Parameters Smith Chart
04 Case Study

Tuner Spacer Design

A tuner upgrade introduced a length mismatch that risked load impedance falling on a stub-swap discontinuity during power ramp. Applied waveguide phase-shifting principles to calculate two optimal spacer lengths, modeled them in SolidWorks, and prepared fabrication drawings. Installation was smooth and the design accommodates future process changes.

RF Engineering Waveguide SolidWorks Phase Shifting
05 Case Study

Automatic Ramp Procedure

Pilot reactor ramp from ignition to full power was manual, variable, and limited to trained operators. Built a LabVIEW SubVI that reads a CSV ramp plan, sequences setpoint changes for power, flow rates, and tuning, and waits for measured values to settle before advancing — with pause and fault-interrupt capability. Ramp is now repeatable and operator-independent.
